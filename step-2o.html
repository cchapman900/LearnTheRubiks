Step 2
First Layer Corners

Number of moves

4

<gif of the move>

Difficulty

Easy

Goal

With our sides solved from the previous layer, we will complete the rest of the first layer with the corners.

<abstract solved 1st layer>

The Technique

This move will take a piece from the bottom layer and move it into position in the top layer without offseting any other corner or side pieces that might already be in position.

<illustration of the move (arrow from bottom piece)>

Starting Position

Before going into the move, we will need to make sure that the target piece we are trying to solve is on the bottom layer facing out towards us.

<correct starting position, and incorrect starting positions>

The Moves

With our white center piece facing up, and the target piece on the bottom layer facing out towards you:

  1. Rotate the bottom layer across from the side it's on
  2. Bring the side it was on down
  3. Bring the bottom layer back over
  4. Rotate that side back up

<grid illustration of each move>

Problem Scenarios

In the case of having the corner facing down, instead of out... or if it is in the top layer, but not matching the side center pieces, we can use the excact same move to "knock" it out of position into hopefully the correct starting position.

<grid illustration of each move>
